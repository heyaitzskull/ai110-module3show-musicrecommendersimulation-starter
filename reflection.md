# Reflection on Profile Comparisons

High-Energy Pop vs Chill Lofi: The high-energy pop profile got mostly upbeat pop tracks with high energy, while the chill lofi one shifted to low-energy, acoustic-heavy songs. It makes sense because the system really focuses on genre and energy, so changing those preferences flips the recommendations completely.

High-Energy Pop vs Deep Intense Rock: Both like high energy, but the pop fan got happy, danceable songs, and the rock lover got intense, aggressive ones. The mood difference explains it – the system groups moods and prioritizes exact matches, so it separates happy from intense even with similar energy.

Conflicting High Energy Sad vs Low Energy Intense: The sad high-energy profile recommended pop songs that were energetic but not too happy, while the intense low-energy one went for rock tracks that were moody despite the energy mismatch. This shows the system struggles with conflicting preferences, favoring genre over energy when they clash.

Extreme Classical Neutral vs Chill Lofi: Classical got orchestral pieces with varying energy, but neutral mood made it pick balanced ones. Lofi stayed chill and low-energy. The genre switch is huge here, proving the system leans hard on genre matching above all else.
